The story Panchparameshwar explores a myriad of sociological ideas, ranging from the tradeoffs one considers during social interactions and decision making to how societal narratives impact humans in a day to day lives. The society constantly tests human beings and their moral compass by forcing them to take complex decisions on a day to day basis. This process of decision making would have been quite simple had there not been a constant conflict between the action's personal utility and moral righteousness. This dilemma might be more philosophical than sociological but the way it's existence manifests in human decision making and behaviour has some larger sociological and behavorial impacts. The existence of this dilemma has been observed thrice in the story: treatment of Khala by Jumman, when Algu is selected as sarpanch in Jumman's hearing and eventually when Jumman is selected as sarpanch in Algu's hearing. The first case in fundamentally different from the others because in the latter cases, a unique narrative of sacredness of the 'position of responsibility' was present. This brings us to the second important sociological idea of how narratives in society drive human behaviour, decision making and have some interesting consequences. Lastly, we shall do a sociological analysis of the Panchayati Raj; specifically it's standing as a local legal system complementary to the larger formal system of the judiciary.
