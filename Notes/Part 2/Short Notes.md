### 2| Patricia - Family Marriage Kinship
https://gemini.google.com/app/8a975aed0019d289

**Key Scholars and their Theories:**
- **Irawati Karve (1965/1953):** Identified four major linguistic-cultural regions of Indian kinship: Indo-Aryan/Sanskritic (northern), Dravidian (southern), central, and eastern. She asserted that the Hindu joint family and the caste system provide unity to India.
- **K.M. Kapadia (1966):** Proposed that the Hindu patrilineal joint family is the unique and universal form of the Indian family.
- **Thomas Trautmann:** Postulated that the overarching principle of Hindu kinship is the ideology of _kanyadana_ (the gift of the virgin).
- **Louis Dumont:** Argued that the unity of Indian society lies in the cultural differentiation between "wife-givers" and "wife-takers" (relations of affinity over relations of descent).
- **M.N. Srinivas:** Prophetically spoke of India polarizing into "dual cultures".

**Important Terminology:**
- **Kanyadana:** The ideology of the "gift of the virgin," considered the purest of all gifts.
- **Stridhan:** The specific portion of dowry gifts intended for the bride herself to control ("woman's property").
- **Isogamy vs. Hypergamy:** Isogamy refers to caste-endogamous marriage between status equals. Hypergamy refers to a woman marrying into a family of superior caste status.
- **Patri-virilocal residence:** The kinship rule meaning a new bride should be brought to live with her husband's family.
- **Stem family:** A restricted joint household comprising a parental couple (or single parent) living with one married son and his children.

**Statistics, Laws, and Facts:**
- **Arranged Marriage Rate:** An estimated 90 percent of marriages in India are arranged.
- **Legal Age of Marriage:** The legal age is 18 for girls and 21 for boys.
- **Marriage Acts:** The Hindu Marriage Act (1955) and the Special Marriage Act (1954) do not give the institution of "arranged marriage" any legal status.
- **Dowry Legislation:** The legislature sought to curb dowry as a "social evil" in 1949, and introduced guest control regulations during the Indian Emergency (1975-1977) to restrict conspicuous spending, but both were largely disregarded.
    

**Structural Hierarchy of the Ideal Indian Family:**
- The parent-son (lineal) relationship is privileged over the husband-wife (conjugal) relationship.
- The brother-brother (collateral) bond is expected to prevail over both parental and conjugal bonds to maintain joint family solidarity.
- The brother-sister bond is considered inviolable, with the brother acting as a lifelong trustee of his sister's welfare.

### 5| Xiang Biao - Producing “IT People” in Andhra

- **The "IT Mania" Was About Jobs, Not Industry:** Despite the political hype generated by Chief Minister Chandrababu Naidu, actual foreign investments in Andhra Pradesh's IT sector were modest, and its share of software exports actually dropped between 1994 and 1999. The real mania was centered on the lucrative overseas IT jobs and the "Dollar Dreams" they represented.
- **Rural Wealth Funded the Global IT Boom:** The substantial investments required for private IT education came largely from the agricultural surplus of rural coastal Andhra. This wealth was primarily diverted by dominant agrarian castes (Kammas and Reddys) who sought to elevate their social and economic status.


### Key Figures and Terms

- **Chandrababu Naidu:** The Chief Minister of Andhra Pradesh (starting in 1995) who prioritized IT development, calling himself the "CEO" of Andhra Pradesh and "ABC" (Andhra's Babu Computer).
- **Cyberabad:** The northeastern zone of Hyderabad designated as an IT-based special-development area.
- **D-shops:** A colloquial term used for private engineering/degree colleges, notoriously known for "selling degrees" without imparting practical knowledge.
- **T-shops:** A colloquial term for private IT training institutes that sold technical skills required for immediate employment.
- **U.S. Rule:** A term used by training institute owners to describe how local course offerings in Hyderabad were entirely dictated by fluctuating technology trends and demands in the United States.
    

### Key Organizations

- **NIIT (National Institute of Information Technology):** Described as the biggest IT training institute in Asia, which secured a crucial "premium education and training partner" status with Microsoft.
- **Baan:** A Dutch software house headquartered in Amsterdam that had an office in Hyderabad.

### 6| Rohit De - Mumtaz Bibi’s broken heart
https://gemini.google.com/app/bc41a5f149fc909d

- **The Strategic Use of _Takhayur_ (Eclecticism):** The paper highlights how the authors of the DMMA utilized _takhayur_, a recognized principle in Islamic jurisprudence allowing for the selective application of rules from different schools of law. This allowed them to apply the more liberal divorce provisions of the Maliki school to Hanafi Muslim women, who traditionally had highly restricted divorce options.
- **Apostasy as a Catalyst for Conservative Support:** A major claim is that conservative ulama supported the bill primarily to stop the growing trend of Muslim women renouncing Islam (apostasy) simply as a legal loophole to dissolve their unhappy marriages.
- **The Fracture Over Secular vs. Religious Authority:** The paper argues that the coalition behind the DMMA fell apart over Clause 6, which would have mandated that only Muslim judges could hear these divorce suits. The rejection of this clause highlighted a profound ideological split regarding who held the ultimate authority to interpret Muslim law in India.

### 8| Anindita Chakrabarti, Suchandra Ghosh - Judicial Reform vs Adjudication of Personal Law
